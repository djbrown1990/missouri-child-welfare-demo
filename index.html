Grant reporting and compliance acceleration

Walkthrough Focus

The problem in plain terms

Teams spend too much time fixing data before they can report.

  • Important case details live in different places and do not always match.
  • Staff manually compare records, which is slow and easy to get wrong.
  • Reporting deadlines are harder to meet when cleanup happens at the last minute.
  • Audit evidence is weaker when decisions are not captured consistently.

Use Case: Streamline grant reporting

Goal: make reporting faster, safer, and easier for operations teams.

What we change

  • Clean and align records before any export is allowed.
  • Stop non-compliant exports and create clear follow-up tasks.
  • Send only approved data packages to AWS evidence storage.

What success looks like

  • Same checks every time, with clear ownership.
  • Caseworkers see exactly what to fix and why.
  • Audit-ready records with timestamps and evidence.

Why current-state fails

The same household can appear differently across Salesforce pages and form sources.

Household / Account Contact Role Case Form Submission External Assertions
  • Address, county, and composition fields drift between household and submitted forms.
  • Role collisions occur when a contact is assigned conflicting workforce/caregiver roles.
  • Case dates and consent flags diverge across intake, external systems, and updates.
  • Missing state IDs and stale placements break downstream eligibility checks.

How matching works

Simple rules first, smart scoring second.

Rule checks (always applied)

  • Required fields and format checks (dates, phone).
  • Exact ID matches when unique IDs are available.
  • Source priority rules decide which value wins.

Smart scoring (when data is messy)

  • Name and address similarity scoring.
  • DOB and role plausibility checks.
  • Clear explanation for every merge recommendation.
The system flags common issues: duplicate people, overlapping case dates, and conflicting role assignments.

Compliance gate

No export goes out until policy checks pass.

  • Checks include required fields, consent, restricted data, date logic, and case status.
  • If a check fails, export is blocked and a remediation task is created.
  • If all checks pass, the package is prepared with audit context.
  • Every decision is timestamped for reporting and audit review.

Solution summary

One flow from messy records to compliant export evidence.
